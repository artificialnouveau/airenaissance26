History of Airbus

The sky belonged to America. Europe built a rival by building it together.

Airbus is the proof behind the proposal: a fragmented Europe, unable to compete one nation at a time, agreed to build together, survived a near-fatal start, and ended up the largest planemaker on earth. The same recipe is what an "Airbus for AI" is asking for. Here is the short version, with the parallel running alongside.

Who made it happen

  

    

      

        
        
Roger Béteille

      

      
"Mister Airbus"

      
The engineer who set the shared fuselage and the work-share deal that gave each country a real stake. Bought the bulbous Super Guppy planes to ferry the parts to Toulouse.

    

    

      

        
        
Henri Ziegler

      

      
First chief executive

      
Won French government backing for the A300 in 1969 and became Airbus Industrie's first CEO. "There would be no Airbus without Henri Ziegler," a later boss said.

    

    

      

        
        
Franz Josef Strauss

      

      
German political muscle

      
The Bavarian heavyweight who pushed the German side and served as chairman. Munich's airport carries his name.

    

    

      

        
        
Chamant & Schiller

      

      
The signatories, May 1969

      
France's transport minister and Germany's economics minister signed the founding agreement at the Paris Air Show. A political decision, not a commercial one.
